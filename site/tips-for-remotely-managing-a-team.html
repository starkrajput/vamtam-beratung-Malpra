Tips for Remotely Managing a Team During COVID-19

Published August 18, 2021 · Digicomplish Insights

The shift to remote work tested every manager. Leading a distributed team well is less about surveillance and more about clarity, trust and communication — building the conditions for people to do great work from anywhere.

Communicate with intention

Over-communicate context and expectations. Set a predictable rhythm of check-ins, keep decisions and updates written down where everyone can find them, and make space for informal connection so the team still feels like a team.

  • Establish clear goals and a regular cadence of check-ins
  • Default to written, searchable updates
  • Use the right tools for chat, video and async work
  • Measure outcomes, not hours online

Lead with trust

Micromanagement erodes morale fast in a remote setting. Give people ownership of outcomes, be explicit about what "done" looks like, and trust them to manage their own time. Autonomy paired with clear accountability is what keeps distributed teams productive.

Protect wellbeing

Remote work blurs the line between home and office. Encourage boundaries, watch for burnout, and check in on people as people — not just on their tasks. A healthy team is a high-performing team, wherever they log in from.
